Projects

This page highlights selected research projects that reflect my interests in neuroepigenetics, chromatin biology, and mechanisms of neuronal vulnerability in disease. Together, these projects span molecular neuroscience, genomics, behavior, and computational modeling. For code and analysis pipelines, see my GitHub.

Doctoral Research

  • Epigenetic Regulation of Lysosomal Function and Memory in Alzheimer’s Disease — University of Toronto (2025– )

    My PhD research investigates how histone variant–mediated chromatin regulation shapes lysosomal gene expression and neuronal vulnerability in Alzheimer’s disease. I focus on the histone variant macroH2A1 as a potential regulator of long-term transcriptional programs that support lysosomal function in hippocampal neurons.

    Using a combination of chromatin profiling (ChIP-seq), transcriptomics (RNA-seq), viral manipulation in vivo, and lysosomal functional assays, I examine how epigenetic dysregulation may contribute to early cellular dysfunction preceding overt neurodegeneration. This work integrates molecular, cellular, and behavioral approaches to link chromatin state to memory-relevant outcomes.

    Core methods: ChIP-seq (in training), RNA-seq (in training), AAV-mediated gene manipulation, confocal microscopy, lysosomal pH and proteolysis assays, rodent behavioral models.

Undergraduate & Fellowship Research

  • Postpartum Depression, Neuroinflammation, and Antidepressant Response — CAMH (2025)

    Conducted independent research on neuroimmune mechanisms underlying postpartum depression in rodent models, examining how inflammatory signaling interacts with SSRI treatment efficacy. Combined behavioral assays with immunohistochemistry to assess microglial plasticity and brain-region–specific effects.

  • Metacommunity Dynamics in Fragmented Landscapes — McMaster University (2024–2025)

    Developed agent-based computational models to test predictions of formal metacommunity theory, focusing on how patch size, connectivity, and species specialization influence biodiversity persistence. This work strengthened my quantitative reasoning and systems-level thinking, which now inform my approach to complex biological networks in neuroscience.

    Code on GitHub
  • Plant Immunity and Transcriptional Regulation — McMaster University (2023–2024)

    Supported molecular biology research on systemic acquired resistance in Arabidopsis thaliana, gaining experience in RNA extraction, PCR, and experimental design. This work provided early training in transcriptional regulation and host defense mechanisms.

  • Amphibian Disease Ecology and Conservation — Operation Wallacea, Honduras (2023)

    Conducted field-based surveillance of chytrid fungal infections in amphibian populations within cloud forest ecosystems. Authored a report examining disease emergence and conservation risk, contributing to broader efforts to understand pathogen-driven biodiversity loss.
