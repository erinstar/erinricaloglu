Scientific Outreach & Communication

Alongside research and teaching, I am committed to making science accessible, engaging, and easier to connect with. My outreach work includes classroom visits, public STEM programming, volunteer coordination, science writing, and presentations for academic and broader audiences.

Outreach Roles

  • Volunteer Coordinator, Let’s Talk Science | Parlons sciences (2025–present)
    Coordinate STEM outreach programs through the University of Toronto Mississauga site, including school visits, community programming, and large-scale youth events. Recruit, train, and support undergraduate volunteers, and help organize activities that make science interactive and approachable for K–12 students.
  • Sci High Graduate Communicator, GTA (2025–present)
    Deliver interactive classroom talks on molecular biology, genetics, and research pathways for high school students. These sessions are designed to make university-level science more approachable and to help students see how research connects to real-world questions.
  • Communications Assistant, Women’s Health Research Cluster (2024–present)
    Create accessible science communication materials on women’s health and reproductive justice. Contribute to infographics, social media content, and knowledge mobilization initiatives that translate research for academic and public audiences.

Science Communication & Presentations

  • Science Communication Blog (2026–present)
    Co-develop a public-facing science blog focused on making research easier to understand without relying on unnecessary jargon. The blog highlights accessible explanations of topics in neuroscience, genomics, health, and biology.
  • Graduate Research Trainee Presentation, University of Toronto (January 2026)
    Presented my first PhD proposal project to graduate students and researchers in the Department of Psychology, sharing the project rationale, research questions, and planned experimental approach.
  • Brain Bee Speaker, University of Toronto (2026)
    Spoke to high school students about my academic journey from high school to doctoral training, sharing experiences in research, neuroscience, and navigating pathways into science.
  • CAMH Research Presentation, University of Toronto Faculty Club (2025)
    Presented summer research on postpartum depression, behavioural neuroscience, and rodent models under the supervision of Dr. Liisa Galea at the Centre for Addiction and Mental Health.
  • Ontario Biology Day Presenter (2025)
    Presented honours thesis research on computational ecology to biology students, faculty, and researchers.
  • McMaster Undergraduate Research Symposium Presenter (2025)
    Shared honours thesis research with an interdisciplinary undergraduate research audience.
  • Senior Thesis Presentation, McMaster University (2025)
    Presented thesis findings on how small habitat patches may support biodiversity under equivalent total habitat area; received the highest grade.
