Projects

This page highlights selected research projects across neuroscience, molecular biology, computational ecology, and conservation. Together, these experiences reflect my interest in how biological systems change across molecular, cellular, behavioural, and ecological scales. For code and analysis pipelines, see my GitHub.

Doctoral Research

  • Epigenetic Regulation of Lysosomal Function in Alzheimer’s Disease
    University of Toronto, 2025–present

    My PhD research examines how histone variant regulation influences gene expression programs in hippocampal neurons during Alzheimer’s disease. I focus on macroH2A1 and its potential role in regulating lysosomal pathways that are important for neuronal function and cellular homeostasis.

    This project uses chromatin profiling, transcriptomic approaches, in vivo viral manipulation, microscopy, and lysosomal assays to study early molecular changes that may contribute to disease progression.

    Core methods: ChIP-seq training, RNA-seq training, AAV-mediated gene manipulation, confocal microscopy, lysosomal functional assays, and rodent behavioural models.

Undergraduate & Fellowship Research

  • Postpartum Depression, Neuroinflammation, and Antidepressant Response
    Centre for Addiction and Mental Health, 2025

    Conducted research on postpartum depression in rodent models under the supervision of Dr. Liisa Galea. This project examined behavioural outcomes and brain-region-specific cellular changes related to antidepressant response and neuroinflammatory signaling.

  • Metacommunity Dynamics in Fragmented Landscapes
    McMaster University, 2024–2025

    Developed agent-based computational models to test how habitat patch size, connectivity, and species specialization influence biodiversity persistence. This honours thesis project used simulation-based approaches to explore how smaller habitat patches may support biodiversity under equivalent total habitat area.

    Code on GitHub
  • Plant Immunity and Systemic Acquired Resistance
    McMaster University, 2023–2024

    Supported molecular biology research on systemic acquired resistance in Arabidopsis thaliana. This role provided early training in RNA extraction, PCR, plant immunity, and experimental design.

  • Primate Welfare and Sanctuary-Based Research
    Fauna Foundation / Central Washington University, 2024

    Contributed to information research related to primate behaviour, welfare, and sanctuary-based care in collaboration with Central Washington University.

  • Amphibian Disease Ecology and Conservation
    Operation Wallacea, Honduras, 2023

    Supported field-based biodiversity and disease ecology work in tropical ecosystems, with a focus on amphibian conservation and pathogen-related threats.
